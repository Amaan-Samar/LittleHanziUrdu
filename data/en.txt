In Karachi, the accused Anmol alias Pinky, who was arrested for drug trafficking, was produced before the court of Judicial Magistrate South. The accused was brought to the City Court with her face covered under tight security, where she was produced before the court.

During the hearing, the accused raised a hue and cry and said that she had been detained for 22 days, that she was arrested from Lahore and brought to Karachi, and that false cases had been made against her. The accused alleged that she was also tortured during her detention.

The court assured the accused that she would not be harassed in court and that her full position would be heard.

Anmol alias Pinky further told the court that 20 to 25 cases are being registered against him and he is being pressured into confessing.

During the hearing, the investigating officer told the court that 7 people have been arrested and recovery is underway on the identification of the accused, however, she is being evasive in the investigation.

Upon the court's inquiry, the investigating officer stated that the initial order regarding the case was available in the police mobile, at which the court expressed its displeasure.

The investigating officer further said that the accused is part of an organized gang, three cases are under investigation against her and data related to her contacts has also been obtained. The court was told that drugs have allegedly been supplied to 800 people and the gang also includes foreigners.

After hearing the arguments of the parties, the court reserved its decision on the accused's request for physical remand.
